Timeline of Irish–Indigenous Interactions

This timeline presents all recorded dataset entries, tracing colonial entanglement, migration systems, displacement, cultural exchange, and modern acts of solidarity between Irish and Indigenous peoples.

1600–1800

Irish Settlement in Indigenous Territories

Irish settlers entered North America through colonial systems, labor migration, and settlement expansion across Indigenous lands.

1801

Acts of Union

British consolidation dissolved Irish parliamentary independence, reinforcing colonial governance structures over Ireland.

1830–1831

Treaty of Dancing Rabbit Creek

Choctaw Nation ceded 11 million acres of land under coercive treaty conditions, initiating forced relocation westward.

1830–1850

Trail of Tears

Systemic forced relocation of Indigenous nations including the Choctaw, Cherokee, and others across the southeastern United States.

1845–1852

Great Irish Famine

Mass starvation and migration caused by potato blight and colonial economic systems disproportionately affecting western Ireland.

1847

Choctaw Nation Famine Donation

The Choctaw Nation raised funds for Irish famine relief despite experiencing displacement from the Trail of Tears.

1847–1848

Irish Famine Relief Distribution

International relief efforts distributed aid across Ireland, including Choctaw contributions.

1850s

Irish Missionary Activity

Irish Catholic missionaries worked in Indigenous communities across North America in education and religious missions.

1860s–1890s

Cross-Cultural Correspondence

Letters, oral histories, and cultural exchange emerged across Irish migrant and Indigenous networks.

1861–1865

American Civil War

Irish immigrants fought in large numbers while Indigenous nations faced continued territorial disruption and division.

1861–1921

James Mooney Ethnographic Work

Irish-American ethnologist James Mooney documented Cherokee and Indigenous cultural systems, preserving oral histories.

1919

Éamon de Valera Honorary Chief

De Valera was named honorary chief during Irish fundraising efforts for independence.

1990

Famine Walk

Choctaw representatives joined Irish commemorations of the Great Famine in County Mayo.

1992

Choctaw Delegation Visit to Ireland

Irish and Choctaw delegations met to commemorate shared histories of displacement and colonial violence.

1992

Dublin Memorial Plaque

A plaque was installed in Dublin recognizing Choctaw famine relief contributions.

1995

Mary Robinson Visit to Choctaw Nation

The Irish president visited the Choctaw Nation as part of continued diplomatic and cultural recognition.

2017

Kindred Spirits Monument

Irish public sculpture commemorating Choctaw famine relief generosity.

2017

Choctaw Delegation Visit to Ireland

Choctaw representatives participated in memorial and cultural exchange ceremonies in Ireland.

2018–2024

Irish–Choctaw Educational Exchange Programs

Ongoing academic and cultural exchange programs connecting Irish institutions and the Choctaw Nation.

2020

Navajo and Hopi COVID-19 Relief

Irish fundraising supported Indigenous pandemic relief efforts in solidarity with Choctaw historical memory.

2020–2021

Pandemic Solidarity Fund

Transnational relief networks expanded during COVID-19 through Irish and Indigenous cooperation.

2024

Eternal Heart Monument

Choctaw Nation unveiled a monument celebrating enduring Irish–Indigenous solidarity.

2025

Navajo and Hopi Gift to Ireland

A woven rug was gifted to Ireland as a symbol of continued reciprocity and solidarity.
